# Audience Variants

This document outlines how the Seed pitch should be tailored for different audiences. While the core vision remains consistent, each audience evaluates Seed through a different lens. Adapting the framing, emphasis, and language increases clarity, credibility, and impact.

---

## 1. Impact Investors & Philanthropic Capital

### Primary Focus
- Financial inclusion
- Social impact
- Equity and access
- Long-term systemic change

### What They Care About
- Who is being helped and why current systems fail them
- Measurable impact outcomes
- Sustainability without dependency on donations
- Ethical design and governance

### How to Frame Seed
- Seed is an offline-first financial infrastructure for the 1+ billion people excluded from traditional banking.
- Emphasize dignity, autonomy, and resilience.
- Highlight group savings, trust scores, and local economic empowerment.
- Position Seed as infrastructure, not charity.

### Key Metrics to Highlight
- Number of unbanked users reached
- Reduction in transaction friction
- Adoption in low-connectivity regions
- Group savings participation rates

### Language Style
- Human-centered
- Mission-driven
- Long-term impact focused

---

## 2. Venture Capital (Early-Stage / Frontier Tech)

### Primary Focus
- Scalability
- Differentiation
- Market size
- Defensibility

### What They Care About
- Why this is technically hard
- Why Seed wins where others fail
- How big the market can become
- What prevents replication

### How to Frame Seed
- Seed is a new financial primitive: offline, mesh-based money.
- Emphasize novel architecture (mesh ledger, offline sync, trust logic).
- Show path from MVP to massive networks.
- Frame early users as beachhead markets.

### Key Metrics to Highlight
- TAM/SAM/SOM
- Network effects
- Cost per device vs value created
- Expansion pathways

### Language Style
- Clear, technical, confident
- Visionary but grounded

---

## 3. Hardware / Deep-Tech Investors

### Primary Focus
- Feasibility
- Reliability
- Manufacturing path
- Power efficiency

### What They Care About
- Hardware risks and mitigations
- Bill of materials
- Power budget
- Environmental durability

### How to Frame Seed
- Seed is a purpose-built financial device optimized for extreme conditions.
- Emphasize low-power design, e-ink, LoRa, secure elements.
- Highlight modularity and iteration path.
- Show roadmap from prototype to manufacturable product.

### Key Metrics to Highlight
- Battery life
- Unit cost at scale
- Failure rates
- Environmental tolerance

### Language Style
- Precise
- Systems-oriented
- Engineering-first

---

## 4. NGOs, Governments, and Development Organizations

### Primary Focus
- Stability
- Trust
- Compliance
- Integration with existing systems

### What They Care About
- Safety and abuse prevention
- Regulatory considerations
- Interoperability
- Pilot feasibility

### How to Frame Seed
- Seed complements existing systems rather than replacing them.
- Emphasize auditability, offline resilience, and governance.
- Show how Seed works in refugee camps, disaster zones, and rural economies.
- Highlight data minimization and privacy-by-design.

### Key Metrics to Highlight
- Deployment readiness
- Training requirements
- Policy alignment
- Pilot success criteria

### Language Style
- Clear
- Conservative
- Risk-aware

---

## 5. Academic Researchers & Technical Advisors

### Primary Focus
- Novelty
- Rigor
- Research value

### What They Care About
- Soundness of algorithms
- Comparison to existing systems
- Open questions
- Ethical implications

### How to Frame Seed
- Seed is a real-world testbed for offline distributed systems.
- Emphasize conflict resolution, trust modeling, and human-centered design.
- Highlight opportunities for collaboration and validation.

### Key Metrics to Highlight
- Algorithmic guarantees
- Failure modes
- Simulation results
- Research extensions

### Language Style
- Analytical
- Precise
- Curious

---

## 6. General Public / Crowdfunding Audiences

### Primary Focus
- Story
- Relatability
- Tangible impact

### What They Care About
- Why Seed exists
- Who it helps
- How they can support it

### How to Frame Seed
- Tell human stories: vendors, families, students.
- Explain Seed without technical jargon.
- Emphasize empowerment and resilience.
- Make the mission emotionally resonant.

### Key Metrics to Highlight
- Real-world use cases
- Early success stories
- Clear next steps

### Language Style
- Simple
- Emotional
- Inspiring
