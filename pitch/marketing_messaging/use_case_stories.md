# Use Case Stories

This document presents concrete, human-centered use cases that demonstrate how Seed operates in real-world environments. These stories are designed to help investors, partners, and stakeholders understand Seed’s value through practical scenarios rather than abstract technology descriptions.

---

## Purpose of Use Case Stories

The goals of these use cases are to:
- Demonstrate real demand for offline-first financial tools
- Show how Seed functions without internet, smartphones, or banks
- Illustrate economic impact at the individual and community level
- Make Seed’s value intuitive to non-technical audiences
- Support investor confidence through realistic adoption narratives

---

## Use Case 1: Rural Market Vendor (Daily Commerce)

### Context
A produce vendor operates in a rural village where:
- There is no reliable internet
- Cash is commonly used but unsafe to store
- Banks are several hours away
- Mobile money services are unavailable or unreliable

### Problem
- Vendor risks theft by carrying cash
- Customers frequently lack exact change
- No record of daily income or expenses
- Cannot save securely or plan financially

### Seed in Action
- Vendor receives payments directly into their Seed device
- Customers pay by transferring value from their own Seed devices
- Transactions sync locally through the mesh network
- Ledger records income automatically
- Vendor checks balance and transaction history on e-ink display

### Outcome
- Reduced cash handling and theft risk
- Faster transactions and better customer trust
- Daily income tracking without literacy barriers
- Immediate improvement in financial stability

## Use Case 2: Group Savings Circle (Community Resilience)

### Context
A group of 10 community members participate in a rotating savings group:
- Each member contributes weekly
- One member receives the pooled amount per cycle
- Trust and transparency are critical
- Disputes are common due to missing records

### Problem
- No formal recordkeeping
- High potential for disputes or fraud
- Difficult to enforce accountability
- Loss of trust collapses the group

### Seed in Action
- Group savings group created on Seed
- Weekly contributions logged automatically
- Trust scores update based on contribution behavior
- Mesh network keeps all members synchronized
- Group members can review contribution history at any time

### Outcome
- Transparent and tamper-resistant savings system
- Increased group participation and longevity
- Reduced conflict and stronger social trust
- Financial inclusion without formal banking

## Use Case 3: Refugee Camp or Disaster Zone (Emergency Finance)

### Context
In a refugee camp or disaster-affected area:
- Infrastructure is damaged or nonexistent
- Power is scarce
- Identity documents may be missing
- Aid distribution is chaotic and inefficient

### Problem
- Cash aid is unsafe and hard to distribute
- No way to track who received assistance
- Fraud and duplication are common
- People lack a secure place to store value

### Seed in Action
- Seed devices distributed with preloaded balances
- Identity secured via fingerprint or device binding
- Aid organizations transfer value device-to-device
- No internet or cellular service required
- Emergency wipe protects users if devices are stolen

### Outcome
- Faster, safer aid distribution
- Reduced fraud and duplication
- Dignity and autonomy for recipients
- Scalable solution for humanitarian operations

## Use Case 4: Small Business Growth (Micro-Entrepreneurship)

### Context
A small tailoring business wants to grow:
- Needs to track revenue and expenses
- Wants access to informal loans
- Operates entirely in cash
- Has no credit history

### Problem
- No financial records to prove reliability
- Cannot access capital
- Savings are insecure
- Business growth is limited

### Seed in Action
- Business transactions logged automatically
- Trust score builds over time based on repayment and activity
- Participates in group lending circles
- Mesh ledger creates a verifiable financial history

### Outcome
- Informal credit access based on behavior, not paperwork
- Improved business planning and growth
- Pathway to larger economic participation
- Foundation for future integration with formal systems

## Use Case 5: Education and Financial Literacy

### Context
A community with low literacy rates:
- Limited access to financial education
- High vulnerability to scams
- Language barriers exist

### Problem
- People do not understand basic financial concepts
- Fear of formal financial systems
- Exploitation is common

### Seed in Action
- AI assistant explains transactions verbally
- Uses local language and simple prompts
- Warns users of suspicious behavior
- Teaches saving habits over time

### Outcome
- Increased financial confidence
- Reduced exploitation
- Long-term behavior change
- Empowerment without dependence on schools or internet
