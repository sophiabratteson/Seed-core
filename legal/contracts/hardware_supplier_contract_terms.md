# Hardware Supplier Contract Terms

This document defines the standard contractual terms between Seed and its hardware suppliers. The purpose of this agreement is to ensure reliable sourcing, quality control, intellectual property protection, ethical manufacturing, and long-term scalability while minimizing operational and regulatory risk.

These terms are designed to support early-stage prototyping as well as future mass production.

---

## 1. Scope of Supply

Suppliers agree to manufacture and/or provide hardware components, subassemblies, or finished devices as specified by Seed.

Covered items may include:
- Printed circuit boards (PCBs)
- Electronic components and modules
- Batteries and power systems
- Enclosures and mechanical parts
- Sensors, secure elements, and radio components
- Assembly and testing services (if applicable)

All supplied items must conform to Seed’s provided specifications, drawings, and documentation.

---

## 2. Specifications and Change Control

- All components must meet Seed’s technical specifications and performance requirements.
- No substitutions, component changes, or process changes are permitted without prior written approval from Seed.
- Suppliers must notify Seed of any end-of-life (EOL) risks or supply constraints as early as possible.
- Approved alternatives must meet or exceed original specifications.

---

## 3. Quality Assurance and Testing

Suppliers must implement and maintain quality assurance processes appropriate to the supplied components.

Minimum requirements include:
- Incoming material inspection
- In-process quality checks
- Final inspection prior to shipment
- Functional testing where applicable

Seed reserves the right to:
- Audit supplier facilities and processes
- Request test reports and quality documentation
- Reject non-conforming goods

---

## 4. Compliance and Certifications

Suppliers must comply with all applicable laws, regulations, and standards, including but not limited to:
- Electrical safety standards
- Radio frequency regulations
- Environmental regulations (e.g., RoHS, REACH)
- Labor and workplace safety laws

Suppliers must provide compliance documentation upon request.

---

## 5. Intellectual Property and Confidentiality

- All designs, specifications, and documentation provided by Seed remain the exclusive property of Seed.
- Suppliers may not use Seed’s designs for any purpose other than fulfilling contractual obligations.
- Reverse engineering, copying, or resale of Seed designs is strictly prohibited.
- Confidential information must be protected and not disclosed to third parties.

---

## 6. Exclusivity and Non-Compete (Optional)

Where agreed:
- Suppliers may be restricted from manufacturing identical or substantially similar products for direct competitors.
- Any exclusivity arrangements must be explicitly defined in writing.

---

## 7. Pricing and Payment Terms

- Pricing must be clearly defined per unit, per batch, or per service.
- Volume-based pricing tiers should be specified where applicable.
- Payment terms may include net 30, net 60, or milestone-based payments.
- Currency, taxes, and shipping costs must be clearly stated.

---

## 8. Lead Times and Delivery

- Suppliers must provide estimated lead times for production and delivery.
- Delivery schedules must be adhered to unless otherwise agreed.
- Late deliveries must be communicated immediately with revised timelines.

Seed reserves the right to seek alternative suppliers in cases of repeated delays.

---

## 9. Packaging, Labeling, and Shipping

Suppliers must:
- Package goods to prevent damage during transit
- Label shipments clearly with part numbers, batch numbers, and quantities
- Comply with international shipping and customs requirements if applicable

---

## 10. Warranty and Defects

Suppliers warrant that all goods:
- Are free from defects in materials and workmanship
- Conform to agreed specifications
- Are suitable for intended use

Defective goods may be repaired, replaced, or refunded at the supplier’s expense.

---

## 11. Liability and Indemnification

Suppliers agree to indemnify Seed against claims arising from:
- Manufacturing defects
- Regulatory non-compliance
- Intellectual property infringement
- Product safety failures attributable to supplied components

---

## 12. Ethical Manufacturing and Sustainability

Suppliers must adhere to ethical manufacturing practices, including:
- No forced or child labor
- Safe working conditions
- Environmental responsibility
- Responsible sourcing of materials

Seed may terminate agreements for violations of ethical standards.

---

## 13. Termination

Either party may terminate the agreement:
- For material breach
- For failure to meet quality or delivery obligations
- For insolvency or legal violations

Termination does not affect confidentiality or IP obligations.

---

## 14. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

- Governing law shall be defined by Seed’s jurisdiction.
- Disputes should first be resolved through good-faith negotiation.
- Arbitration or legal proceedings may be used if necessary.

---

## 15. Scalability and Future Production

Suppliers acknowledge that Seed intends to scale production over time.

Suppliers should:
- Disclose capacity limitations
- Support ramp-up planning
- Assist with cost optimization and process improvements

---

## 16. Entire Agreement

This document, along with any referenced appendices or purchase orders, constitutes the entire agreement between Seed and the supplier unless otherwise amended in writing.

---

## 17. Summary

These contract terms protect Seed’s technology, ensure manufacturing reliability, and create a foundation for ethical, scalable, and compliant hardware production. They are structured to support both early-stage prototyping and long-term global deployment.
