# 1. Definitions

## Total Addressable Market (TAM)
The total number of people globally who *could* use Seed based on:
- No/low internet access  
- No access to formal financial institutions  
- Heavy reliance on cash  
- Lack of affordable mobile data or devices  
- Absence of existing mobile-money dominance  

## Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)
The subset of TAM located in:
- Regions where Seed can realistically operate in early deployment  
- Countries with compatible radio spectrum regulations  
- Environments where NGOs, microfinance orgs, or local governments could support adoption  

## Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM)
The realistic share Seed can capture in:
- Early pilot countries  
- Within the first 3‚Äì5 years of launch  
- Given limited resources, distribution channels, and B2B/B2G partnerships

# 2. Key Filters to Remove Competitor-Served Populations

## Competitor Categories That Seed *Excludes* From TAM
- Mobile-money mature markets (M-Pesa, bKash, MTN MoMo, GCash, etc.)
- Populations with:
  - Smartphones
  - Stable mobile data
  - Active SIM networks
  - Access to a bank or neobank
  - Cryptocurrency users or digital-wallet users

## Core Criteria for Seed‚Äôs Real Market  
Seed focuses on:
- Low-income rural communities  
- Offline environments  
- Regions with:
  - Electricity scarcity  
  - No reliable cell towers  
  - No stable banking presence  
  - No SIM card registration infrastructure  
  - No digital identity systems
