# User Personas for Seed
This document defines Seed’s primary user personas across emerging markets, rural regions, and underserved urban communities. Personas help clarify real-world needs, constraints, motivations, and adoption behaviors, ensuring Seed’s design and roadmap remain grounded in actual user experience.

---

# 1. Persona: The Micro-Merchant (“Amina”)
## Profile
- Age: 24–45  
- Occupation: Street vendor, market stall owner, food seller  
- Income: Highly variable, cash-dominant  
- Connectivity: Limited or zero internet; periodic access via community WiFi  
- Device Access: Often no smartphone; relies on basic feature phone or nothing  

## Pain Points
- No safe place to keep daily earnings  
- Difficulty accessing capital for inventory  
- No transaction records → no creditworthiness  
- Vulnerable to theft, counterfeit currency, predatory lenders  

## Goals
- Secure daily earnings  
- Access small rotating loans for inventory  
- Build financial identity and stable income  
- Transact with customers safely  

## How Seed Helps
- Secure offline wallet for storing value  
- Group savings + rotating credit within her vendor community  
- Trust-score system to unlock micro-loans  
- Offline transactions with customers  
- Transaction history for future credit opportunities

# 2. Persona: The Daily Laborer (“Joseph”)
## Profile
- Age: 18–50  
- Occupation: Construction worker, transport helper, delivery runner  
- Income: Paid in cash daily  
- Connectivity: Very limited; cannot rely on a smartphone  
- Device Access: Often shares phones with family  

## Pain Points
- No ability to save securely  
- Money often spent immediately out of necessity or theft concerns  
- No access to financial education  
- No identity documentation  

## Goals
- Store earnings safely  
- Save for emergencies  
- Begin building a financial profile  
- Learn simple budgeting  

## How Seed Helps
- Offline savings tool powered by hand-crank and solar  
- Simple AI financial coach that works offline and in local languages  
- No ID required → fingerprint identity on device  
- Group trust networks allow him to join rotating savings pools  

---

# 3. Persona: The Refugee or Displaced Individual (“Maya”)
## Profile
- Age: 14–65  
- Situation: Fleeing conflict, environmental crisis, or economic collapse  
- Connectivity: None; camps often lack power, internet  
- Device Access: No device or unreliable shared device  

## Pain Points
- Loss of all financial documentation  
- No safe way to store aid distributions  
- Difficulty proving identity  
- High vulnerability to exploitation  

## Goals
- Identity reconstruction  
- Secure access to humanitarian aid  
- Ability to store and transfer value within camps  
- Access small credit to rebuild livelihood  

## How Seed Helps
- Fingerprint-secured identity reconstruction stored locally  
- Offline, tamper-proof transaction ledger  
- Aid groups can distribute value directly into Seeds  
- No SIM card, no internet, no phone needed  

---

# 4. Persona: The Rural Farmer (“Samuel”)
## Profile
- Age: 30–70  
- Occupation: Smallholder farmer  
- Income: Seasonal, volatile  
- Connectivity: Extremely limited; power outages common  
- Device Access: Basic phone or none  

## Pain Points
- Cannot save long-term  
- No credit for seeds, fertilizer, or equipment  
- Middlemen exploit pricing due to lack of bargaining power  
- Irregular access to markets and stable cash flow  

## Goals
- Save gradually for agricultural inputs  
- Access low-interest loans during planting season  
- Receive payments from buyers digitally  
- Keep records for financial negotiation  

## How Seed Helps
- Offline wallet with long battery life  
- Group savings among farmers  
- Trust-score loans for agricultural investment  
- Ability to sync transaction history whenever a Seed device passes by another

# 5. Persona: Urban Informal Worker (“Fatima”)
## Profile
- Age: 20–45  
- Occupation: Housekeeper, nanny, informal services  
- Income: Cash-based, unstable  
- Connectivity: Periodic internet access but unreliable devices  
- Device Access: May have smartphone but power outages disrupt usage  

## Pain Points
- Lack of financial stability  
- No emergency savings  
- Debt cycles due to high-interest informal loans  
- Difficulty sending money to family  

## Goals
- Emergency fund  
- Stable budgeting  
- Safer remittances  

## How Seed Helps
- Offline savings wallet with AI budgeting tips  
- Low-fee offline transaction sending/receiving  
- Trust-score–based micro-loans at fair rates  

---

# 6. Persona: Humanitarian Worker / NGO Coordinator (“Luis”)
## Profile
- Age: 28–55  
- Occupation: NGO operations, humanitarian logistics  
- Connectivity: Limited during field missions  
- Device Access: Laptop or smartphone but infrastructure unreliable  

## Pain Points
- Cash-heavy aid distribution slows down delivery  
- Theft, leakage, or corruption risks in cash-based support  
- Difficulty tracking distributions across dispersed populations  
- Verification of beneficiaries is challenging  

## Goals
- Efficient, secure, transparent aid distribution  
- Low infrastructure demands  
- Fraud resistance  
- Auditability  

## How Seed Helps
- Device-to-device syncing enables distribution even with 0 internet  
- Secure ledger ensures tamper resistance  
- Per-device identity systems reduce fraud  
- Automated checks and trust logs provide real-time oversight

# 7. Persona: Community Savings Group Leader (“Beatrice”)
## Profile
- Age: 25–60  
- Role: Organizes local rotating savings groups (ROSCA, VSLA)  
- Connectivity: Limited but acts as a central peer “hub”  
- Device Access: Moderate; often the most digitally literate in her group  

## Pain Points
- Manual bookkeeping is error-prone  
- Tracking contributions is difficult  
- Trust issues occasionally arise  
- Loss/theft of cash destroys group stability  

## Goals
- Maintain accurate records  
- Improve transparency  
- Grow trust and scale group activities  

## How Seed Helps
- Automated group savings logic  
- Transparent contribution logs  
- Offline syncing means she can help update members’ Seeds by walking through the community  
- Improved trust → more savings → greater community economic resilience  

---

# 8. Persona: Local Shop Owner (“Tunde”)
## Profile
- Age: 25–50  
- Business: Small retail shop, groceries, telecom cards  
- Connectivity: Some WiFi access but unreliable  
- Device Access: Smartphone but not used for banking  

## Pain Points
- Cash management is risky  
- No point-of-sale infrastructure  
- Cannot offer digital payments  
- No credit line for inventory expansion  

## Goals
- Accept digital payments  
- Access small business loans  
- Reduce cash handling risks  

## How Seed Helps
- Offline POS-like Seed transactions  
- Strong identity system for credit worthiness  
- Ability to sync with customers and suppliers through mesh network  

---

# 9. Persona: Government/Regulator (“Dr. K.”)
## Profile
- Role: Central bank, rural development ministry, financial inclusion programs  
- Connectivity: Good  
- Device Access: Full tech suite  

## Pain Points
- Rural populations remain unbanked despite national initiatives  
- Cash programs create inefficiencies and corruption  
- Difficulty measuring the true economy  

## Goals
- Expand financial inclusion  
- Enable secure digital value transfer for underserved populations  
- Build financial identities for unbanked citizens  

## How Seed Helps
- Government can seed initial value safely and cheaply  
- Offline-first ensures inclusion of remote populations  
- Strong cryptographic audit trails  

---

# 10. Persona: Future Investor / Corporate Partner (“Olivia”)
## Profile
- VC, Foundation, Telecom, Fintech, Social Impact Investor  

## Pain Points
- Hard to find scalable, infrastructure-light financial solutions  
- Many fintechs require internet and smartphones  
- Offline-first finance is rare and high-impact  

## Goals
- Back globally scalable financial inclusion tools  
- Support sustainable economic growth in developing regions  
- See clear path to commercialization  

## How Seed Helps
- Uniquely offline-first model  
- Clear scalability  
- Ability to plug into banking, mobile money, NGOs, and local economies  

---

# Summary Persona Map

- Micro-merchants → Core early adopters  
- Daily laborers → High impact group  
- Rural farmers → Seasonal economic uplift  
- Refugees → Critical need for identity + cash safety  
- Urban informal workers → Financial stability  
- Community leaders → Adoption accelerators  
- NGOs → Institutional partners  
- Local shops → Payment ecosystem builders  
- Governments → Macro-scale inclusion  
- Investors → Funding and scale  

Seed’s product roadmap should be driven by these personas, ensuring that every feature solves a real, high-impact problem.
