# Field Notes Template – Seed User Research

## Document Purpose

This template standardizes how Seed researchers, founders, and partners collect qualitative and contextual data during field research, interviews, pilots, and observational studies. It ensures insights gathered across regions, cultures, and environments are comparable, actionable, and ethically collected.

This document is used during:
- In-person interviews
- Pilot deployments
- Observational studies
- NGO or partner-led research
- Early adopter trials
- Community onboarding sessions

---

## 1. Session Metadata

Record this information before starting the session.

- **Date**
- **Time**
- **Location (Country / Region / Community)**
- **Environment Type**
  - Rural / Urban / Informal settlement / Refugee camp / Disaster zone / Other
- **Researcher Name**
- **Session Type**
  - Interview / Observation / Pilot usage / Training session / Follow-up
- **Language Used**
- **Translator Used (if any)**

---

## 2. Participant Profile (Non-Identifying)

Do **not** record personally identifiable information.

- **Participant Role**
  - Vendor / Customer / Group savings member / Community leader / NGO worker
- **Approximate Age Range**
- **Gender (Optional / Self-described)**
- **Primary Livelihood**
- **Financial Tools Currently Used**
  - Cash only
  - Informal savings group
  - Mobile money
  - Bank account
  - Combination
- **Technology Access**
  - Feature phone / Smartphone / None
- **Literacy Level**
  - Reads comfortably / Reads with difficulty / Non-literate

---

## 3. Contextual Observations

Capture what is happening **around** the participant.

- Physical environment (noise, lighting, weather, crowding)
- Power availability (electricity present? reliable?)
- Network availability (cellular / internet)
- Social context (alone, with family, with customers)
- Security concerns observed
- Cultural norms affecting interaction or finance

---

## 4. Current Financial Behavior

Describe how money is handled today.

- How does the participant earn income?
- How do they store money?
- How do they send or receive payments?
- How do they save?
- How do they borrow or lend?
- What happens during emergencies?
- What are common risks (theft, loss, fraud)?

---

## 5. Pain Points Identified

List specific challenges mentioned or observed.

- Trust issues
- Access barriers
- Cost barriers
- Time delays
- Safety concerns
- Exclusion from formal systems
- Complexity or confusion
- Dependence on intermediaries

Use direct quotes where possible.

---

## 6. Introduction of Seed Concept (If Applicable)

If Seed was introduced during the session:

- How was Seed explained?
- Initial participant reaction
- Points of excitement
- Points of confusion
- Immediate concerns or skepticism
- Features that resonated most
- Features that felt unnecessary or unclear

---

## 7. Hands-On Interaction Notes (If Applicable)

If the participant interacted with a prototype or demo:

- Ability to navigate interface
- Understanding of balances and transactions
- Comfort with buttons or input methods
- Reaction to e-ink display
- Reaction to fingerprint or identity mechanism
- Questions asked during use
- Errors or misunderstandings observed

---

## 8. Trust and Social Dynamics

Document how trust is formed and maintained.

- Who does the participant trust financially?
- Role of community groups or leaders
- Existing group savings behavior
- Willingness to participate in shared financial tools
- Concerns about surveillance or tracking
- Reaction to trust score concepts

---

## 9. Group Savings & Lending Insights

If group finance is discussed:

- Existing group size
- Contribution frequency
- Rotation or payout rules
- Conflict resolution methods
- Enforcement mechanisms
- Reaction to Seed group savings features
- Perceived fairness and transparency

---

## 10. Risks and Ethical Considerations

Identify potential harms or sensitivities.

- Safety risks for participant
- Risk of coercion or misuse
- Gender or power imbalances
- Risk of exclusion
- Misinterpretation of Seed’s purpose
- Regulatory or legal concerns raised

---

## 11. Key Insights Summary

Summarize the most important takeaways.

- Top 3 unmet needs
- Top 3 opportunities for Seed
- Major adoption blockers
- Unexpected findings
- Cultural or regional considerations

---

## 12. Actionable Recommendations

Translate insights into action.

- Feature changes
- Interface changes
- Messaging changes
- Training needs
- Policy or governance adjustments
- Pilot design changes

---

## 13. Follow-Up Required

- Additional interviews needed?
- Revisit participant?
- Community-level follow-up?
- Partner engagement required?

---

## 14. Researcher Reflection

For internal use only.

- What surprised you?
- What assumptions were challenged?
- What did not work in this session?
- What would you do differently next time?

---

## 15. Consent & Ethics Checklist

Confirm compliance:

- Informed consent obtained
- Purpose clearly explained
- No PII recorded
- Participant free to withdraw
- No compensation coercion
- Data stored securely
