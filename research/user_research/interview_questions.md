# User Research Interview Questions

This document defines the structured interview framework used to understand user needs, behaviors, constraints, and trust dynamics for Seed. These interviews focus on individuals operating in low-connectivity, cash-based, or informal financial environments, as well as facilitators such as vendors, community leaders, and NGOs.

The goal is to validate Seed’s assumptions, uncover unmet needs, identify risks, and inform product design, trust mechanisms, and rollout strategy.

---

## 1. Interview Objectives

- Understand how people currently store, transfer, and safeguard money
- Identify pain points with cash, banks, mobile money, or informal systems
- Learn how trust is built and enforced without formal institutions
- Evaluate openness to offline digital financial tools
- Assess literacy, language, and accessibility requirements
- Gather feedback on group savings and shared financial tools
- Identify barriers to adoption and potential misuse

---

## 2. Target Interview Groups

### Primary Users
- Informal workers and vendors
- Rural residents with limited internet access
- Refugees or displaced individuals
- Individuals without government-issued IDs
- Users of informal savings groups

### Secondary Stakeholders
- Small business owners
- Community leaders
- NGO field workers
- Aid distribution coordinators
- Microfinance facilitators

---

## 3. Interview Format

- Duration: 30–45 minutes
- Setting: In-person preferred; phone/audio acceptable
- Language: Native language whenever possible
- Recording: With consent only
- Incentives: Small compensation where culturally appropriate

---

## 4. Background Questions

- Can you describe what you do for work or how you earn money?
- How do you usually receive payments?
- How do you usually pay for goods or services?
- Do you use cash, mobile money, banks, or something else?
- How often do you have access to the internet or electricity?

---

## 5. Money Storage & Safety

- Where do you keep your money today?
- Have you ever lost money due to theft, damage, or misplacement?
- Do you feel your current method is safe? Why or why not?
- What worries you most about keeping money?
- Have you ever trusted someone else to hold money for you?

---

## 6. Sending & Receiving Money

- How do you send money to others today?
- How far do people usually travel to exchange money?
- What problems do you face when sending or receiving money?
- Have you ever been unable to complete a transaction? Why?
- How important is speed vs reliability when transferring money?

---

## 7. Trust & Reputation

- How do you decide who to trust with money?
- Have you ever been cheated or not paid back?
- How are disputes usually resolved?
- Do people have reputations for being reliable or unreliable?
- Would a shared reputation or trust score be helpful or harmful?

---

## 8. Group Savings & Loans

- Are you part of a savings group or rotating fund?
- How does the group keep track of contributions?
- What happens when someone doesn’t pay?
- What works well about the current system?
- What problems or conflicts happen most often?

---

## 9. Technology Comfort & Accessibility

- Do you own a phone? If yes, what kind?
- Can you read and write comfortably?
- Which languages do you prefer?
- Are voice instructions useful?
- Would fingerprint or physical buttons feel easier than typing?

---

## 10. Reaction to Seed Concept

(Describe Seed verbally using simple language before asking)

- Does this idea make sense to you?
- What do you like most about it?
- What concerns you?
- Would you trust it with your money?
- What would stop you from using it?

---

## 11. Offline & Emergency Scenarios

- What happens if power is unavailable for days?
- What happens during conflict, disaster, or displacement?
- Have you ever had to flee quickly with money?
- How important is anonymity or privacy in these situations?
- Would an emergency wipe or hidden account be useful?

---

## 12. Adoption & Rollout

- Who would you trust to introduce this system?
- Would training be needed?
- Would you prefer individual or group onboarding?
- Would incentives encourage adoption?
- How should problems or support requests be handled?

---

## 13. Closing Questions

- If you could change one thing about money today, what would it be?
- Is there anything we didn’t ask that matters to you?
- Would you be willing to test this system in the future?
- May we follow up with you again?

## 15. Ethical Considerations

- Participation must be voluntary
- No financial promises or guarantees
- No collection of sensitive personal data
- Respect cultural norms
- Protect participant anonymity

---

## 16. How Findings Will Be Used

- Inform MVP feature prioritization
- Refine trust score logic
- Adjust UI/UX for accessibility
- Validate market assumptions
- Support investor and partner discussions

---

## 17. Summary

These interviews ensure Seed is built from real human needs, not assumptions. They anchor the system in lived experience, trust dynamics, and practical constraints, increasing the likelihood of adoption, impact, and long-term sustainability.
